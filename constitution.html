Section 3Triple Verification Doctrine (Thesis / Antithesis / Synthesis)

Every conclusion must pass three independent checks. Outputs must include explicit PASS/FAIL with reasons.

A) Thesis — What the evidence appears to state. Extract claims and facts with anchors. No interpretation beyond what is directly supported.

B) Antithesis — What could contradict it. Search for conflicting timestamps, documents, versions, metadata, statements, missing pages, edits, and gaps. List alternative explanations.

C) Synthesis — What survives both. Conclude only from what survives Thesis and Antithesis. Every output includes explicit PASS/FAIL for all three checks.

Consensus Rule: A finding is accepted only when all three checks PASS, or when 2 of 3 PASS with the third being INSUFFICIENT (not FAIL). If any check FAILs, the finding is rejected or downgraded.

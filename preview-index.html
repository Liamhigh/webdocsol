Compliance & Recognition

Institutions That Govern the Seal

Verum Omnis does not operate in a vacuum. Every seal, every hash, every report is built on internationally recognized standards and legally binding frameworks. These are not optional certifications — they are the standards against which evidence is prepared for court admissibility review.

International Standard

ISO/IEC 27037:2012

The international standard for digital evidence collection, preservation, and processing. Verum Omnis complies at every stage — from ingestion (acquisition) to preservation to analysis. This is the gold standard for handling digital evidence across jurisdictions.

Verum Omnis Application

Verum Omnis complies at all four Daubert criteria: testability, peer review, known error rates, and general acceptance. The Nine-Brain Architecture provides continuous internal peer review through voting quorums, ensuring replicable, court-admissible results.

South African Law

Electronic Communications & Transactions Act 25 of 2002

Sections 11-15 of the ECT Act grant cryptographically secured electronic records a strong presumption of data integrity and legal admissibility. Electronic records that are cryptographically secured carry the same weight as paper originals.

Verum Omnis Application

Verum Omnis seals are designed to fall within these provisions, so that cryptographically sealed digital documents can be presented as original electronic evidence under the Act. Admissibility remains a matter for the presiding court.

US Federal Evidence Standard

Daubert Standard

The legal standard used by US federal courts to evaluate scientific and technical evidence. It requires testability, peer review, known error rates, and general acceptance within the relevant scientific community.

Verum Omnis Application

Verum Omnis satisfies all four Daubert criteria through deterministic hashing (testability), Nine-Brain consensus (peer review), quantifiable confidence levels (known error rates), and ISO/IEC 27037 compliance (general acceptance).

Decentralized Proof Anchoring

OpenTimestamps (Bitcoin Blockchain)

OpenTimestamps is an open standard for creating cryptographic timestamp proofs on the Bitcoin blockchain — a public, immutable, decentralized ledger maintained by thousands of nodes worldwide. Once a hash is anchored, the timestamp is permanent and publicly verifiable — entirely independent of any state or corporate authority.

Verum Omnis Application

The triple-hash seal (content hash + metadata hash + constitutional HMAC) is anchored to Bitcoin via OpenTimestamps. This creates permanent, decentralized, time-stamped proof of a document's exact state — no institution can alter or delete it.

Archival Standard

PDF/A-3B Archival Standard (ISO 19005)

The international standard for long-term preservation of electronic documents. PDF/A-3B files are self-contained, self-authenticating, and guarantee that the document will render identically decades from now.

Verum Omnis Application

Every Verum Omnis report is exported as PDF/A-3B with embedded metadata, visible watermarks, and a QR code containing the full verifiable hash string. Any independent third party can recalculate the hashes to confirm integrity.

Mathematical Proof Engine

SHA-512 Cryptographic Algorithm

SHA-512 operates on a 512-bit state size, meaning the probability of a hash collision is 2^-256 — effectively zero. Any modification of a single bit within a file instantly changes the output hash, rendering tampering immediately detectable.

Verum Omnis Application

This is not a claim. This is mathematics. Changing a single comma, a single pixel, or even a single bit produces an entirely different 128-character hash. The mathematics cannot be argued with in court.
